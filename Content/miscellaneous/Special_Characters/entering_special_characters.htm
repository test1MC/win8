Special Characters

Warning: As well as being a rather technical Topic, screen reader users should expect unusual results when viewing the accompanying tables, because they essentially provide a partial list of Unicode characters.

Note: Some characters in this section may not display. This is entirely dependent on the version of Windows you are running, and what Fonts are installed. As a general rule of thumb, if a character will not display here, it will not display in documents within DBT or other programs on this computer either.

Word for Windows:

Many Word files are using special characters for things such as bullet boints, and even telephone symbols, such as ☎ ✄ ☀ ♬. Please familiarize yourself with DBT's Global: Word Importer Hex Value option in order to assist in identifying the Unicode value of such characters.

See also: Troubleshooting: Word - Missing or wrong characters.

Languages:

Those who require to reproduce Latin accented characters in text may find it useful to study the DBT Supported Characters - Alt Method Topic.

Methods of inputting special characters:
  • In DBT - From the Alt Method list:
  1. Hold down the Alt Key, and on the Numeric Pad, type just the four numbers which follow "WIN:####"
  2. Hold down the Alt key and press F9. In the dialog box which appears, type in the DUSCI number and letters which follow "D+##" or "D+####"
  • In a Windows application such as Word for Windows. - From the Alt Method list:
  1. Hold down the Alt Key, and on the Numeric Pad, type just the four numbers which follow "WIN:####"
  • In DBT - From the U+## lists:
  1. Hold down the Ctrl key, and press ] (right square bracket). Type in the number and letters which appear in the DUSCI column.
  • Note: You may or may not see the actual character on the DBT screen. The column in the tables headed Duxbury indicates which character will be shown. In some cases an asterisk or a hash/pound sign will appear.
  • In Word for Windows 2003 upwards - From the U+## lists: (And some other Windows applications)
  1. Enter the four digit alpha-numeric Unicode value.
  2. Press Alt + x immediately afterwards.
    • Note: This method of entering Unicode characters only available in Word 2003, and is dependent on various other factors such as which International version of Windows you have installed, and what languages are supported.
    • You cannot use this method in any other earlier version of Word or Windows.
