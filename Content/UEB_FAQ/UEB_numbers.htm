How do I get dot 5 for a space between numbers?

This is a tip which may also apply in many circumstances.

A telephone number would be written as normal:

123 456 7890

Prior to UEB, the result in braille would usually be:

#abc #def #ghij

In UEB the same should translate to:

#abc"def"ghij

In order to produce the Dot 5 character, you need to insert a "Hard Space" in your text file, also known as a "non-breaking space", before you translate.

If you are working within DBT, this kind of space can be inserted with the keystroke Alt+F2

In Word for Windows, the keystroke is Ctrl+Shift+Space

You can also use this feature to prevent text breaking over 2 lines.
