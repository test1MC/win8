A buffer is the memory in your printer or embosser where data can be stored while it waits to be output. A large buffer is better than a small one. Your computer will quickly send data until the buffer is full, and after that, it will handshake to keep the buffer full. Of course, the computer can only send data as fast as the printer will print it. When a computer is busy handshaking, other operations are slowed down, or possibly even halted.
