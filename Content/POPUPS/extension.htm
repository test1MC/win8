An extension is the portion of a file's name that comes after a period. Within MS-DOS (and Windows), a file may have no extension (e.g. MYFILE), or may have an extension of up to three characters (e.g. MYFILE.DOC). Extensions often help to identify what kind of data is stored in a file. For DBT, and other Duxbury products, typical extensions include:

DXB

DXP

TXT

BRU

BRF
